Marsaxlokk

The Marsaxlokk Fish Market is one of Malta’s most iconic fish markets, situated in the traditional fishing village of Marsaxlokk. Known for its vibrant atmosphere, this market offers a variety of fresh, locally caught fish and attracts both locals and tourists. Its commitment to supporting wild-caught fish aligns with the area’s rich fishing heritage, allowing consumers to directly support local fishermen and experience the true taste of Maltese seafood.
Opening times: 8:30 am–2 pm
Address: 79 Xatt is-Sajjieda, Marsaxlokk

Pixkerija

Pixkerija, Malta’s wholesale fish market, is a primary source of fresh fish for retailers and restaurants across the island. Located in Valletta, it ensures that locally caught seafood reaches consumers, supporting Malta’s fishing industry and reducing reliance on imported or farmed fish. By sourcing fish directly from Maltese waters, Pixkerija provides quality seafood while promoting sustainability in the local food supply chain.
Opening times: 4pm - late evenings
Address: Troubridge Road, Marsa

Birkirkara Village Market

The Birkirkara Village Market offers a unique blend of traditional Maltese goods, including fresh fish from local fishermen. It serves as a community hub, providing consumers with an alternative to farmed fish by offering seasonal, sustainably caught seafood. This market promotes a connection to Malta’s culinary traditions and is a valuable resource for consumers interested in responsibly sourced fish.
Opening times: 6:30 - 11 am on wednesdays and fridays.
Address: WF28+VC5, Tumas Fenech St, Birkirkara
